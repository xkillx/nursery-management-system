# 12-Month Fixed-Term Contract

The commercial relationship between a nursery site and a parent for one child is a 12-month fixed-term advance-pay contract called a Term. Each Term fixes a weekly schedule of session types (via the existing Booking Pattern) and a single hourly rate (snapshotted from the branch rate at term creation). Terms have a hard end at `term_start_date + 12 months - 1 day` and must be explicitly renewed; there is no implicit rollover. In-term changes follow a 1-month notice rule: decreases are free, increases require manager approval (room capacity + staff-to-child ratios). Termination follows the same 1-month notice: the parent pays for the notice month only and the child may leave earlier in that month. The system advances a Term through five derived statuses: `pre_term` (created, settling-in window before start), `active` (within the 12-month range), `pending_renewal` (within 30 days of `term_end_date`, set by the daily scheduler), `ended` (reached `term_end_date + 1 day` with no renewal), `terminated` (ended early via the 1-month notice flow). If a term ends without a renewal recorded, the expire-terms scheduler flips the child to inactive.

The trade-off is that 12-month fixed terms are stricter than rolling subscriptions. They match UK nursery commercial norms, they make advance invoicing tractable (the schedule and rate are stable for the 12 months), and they remove ambiguity at the renewal boundary. They would be a wrong fit for a setting where parents need week-to-week flexibility; the plan leaves that to a future "rolling subscription" mode. The 1-month notice rule on decreases and the manager-approval gate on increases are deliberately conservative; either could be relaxed in a future revision once the operational data is in.

A renewed Term has `term_start_date = previous_term_end_date + 1 day` (no settling-in gap) and is created via the same `POST /api/v1/children/:child_id/terms` route as any other term, with the parent actor reading the previous term's `term_end_date` from the GET endpoints. The renewal surface (a list of `pending_renewal` terms with a one-click "Create renewal term" action) is a web concern; the API is unchanged.
